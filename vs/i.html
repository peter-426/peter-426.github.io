Dr Peter Hebden

Research area: comparative genomics and virtual screening.

Research project: antibiotic drug discovery at Demuris Ltd.

Bacteria come in many shapes (and colors thanks to the dyes used to help study them), as these pictures show. Some are helpful while are others are pathogenic and may cause illness or even death. The development of antibiotics was one of the biggest medical achievements of the 20th centrury. Unfortunately, some pathogenic bacteria have evolved such that they can resist penecillin and many other antibiotics. Their impact on health is the subject of these excellent videos . For more general information, Wikipedia provides an extensive article about bacteria.

In recent research we used comparative genomics to identify bacterial proteins that are probably good drug targets, virtual screening to identify chemical compounds that bind strongly to those proteins and, finally, we selected chemical compounds for testing in the lab where they can be measured for their antimicrobial properties.
